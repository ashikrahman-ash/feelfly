  • What is the difference between digital marketing and digital advertising?

    Digital marketing is a broader term that encompasses all the digital strategies used to promote a brand. It includes a wide range of tactics, such as search engine optimization (SEO), content marketing, social media marketing (SMM), email marketing, and influencer marketing.

    On the other hand, digital advertising refers specifically to the paid promotion of a product, service or brand through digital channels. There are different online advertising methods such as display ads, social media ads, search engine marketing (SEM), and video ads.

    In short, while digital marketing involves a wide range of tactics aimed at building relationships with customers, digital advertising is focused on immediate conversions and involves paid promotion through digital channels.

  • How can I measure the success of my digital campaign?

    We constantly and thoroughly check the performance of every campaign to determine whether it is going to achieve the objectives or need optimization.

    Here are some key performance indicators (KPIs) that you can use to measure the success of your digital marketing campaign as a beginner:

    Web Traffic: You can use tools like Google Analytics to track the number of visits, pageviews, and bounce rate.

    Conversion Rate: Conversion rate measures the percentage of visitors who take a desired action e.g. making a purchase, filling out a form, or subscribing to a newsletter. It is calculated by dividing the number of conversions by the number of visitors.

    Cost Per Acquisition (CPA): CPA measures how much it costs to acquire a new customer or lead. You can calculate your CPA by dividing the total cost of the campaign by the number of conversions.

    Social Media Engagement: If your campaign involves social media, you can measure engagement metrics such as likes, comments, shares, and followers.

    Email Metrics: For email marketing, you can measure metrics such as open rates, click-through rates, and conversion rates.

  • How do you run a digital marketing project?

    Although the digital marketing strategy varies from business to business, the process is usually the same.

    Initial Consultation: First we arrange a meeting to discuss your goals, expectations, and budget so we can understand your requirements and recommend the most suitable services.

    Proposal and Agreement: After the initial consultation, we provide a detailed proposal outlining the services to be provided, timelines, and costs. Once you approve the proposal, we sign an agreement to initiate the project.

    Onboarding: In this stage, we gather all the necessary information from you, such as brand guidelines, social media accounts details, website credentials, and any other relevant information needed to execute the project.

    Strategy Development: Based on the information gathered, we develop a comprehensive digital marketing strategy tailored to your goals and objectives.

    Implementation: Then we start executing the digital marketing strategy across multiple channels, such as social media, search engines, email marketing, and other relevant platforms.

    Monitoring and Analysis: We closely monitor the performance of every marketing campaign and analyze the data to optimize the strategies for better results.

    Reporting: We provide regular reports to you outlining the performance of the campaign, including key metrics such as traffic, engagement, and conversions.

    Ongoing Support: We continue to provide after-sale assistance, including making updates and adjustments to the strategy according to the performance and feedback.
